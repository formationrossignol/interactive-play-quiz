# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Anyone running a live interactive session for a group — teachers and trainers, event and team hosts, community organizers. No single primary audience: the same host-joins-via-code flow serves a classroom exam as easily as a corporate icebreaker or a community meetup poll. The common context is standing in front of a group (physical or remote) and needing everyone's phone to sync to one shared moment right now.

## Product Purpose

Brivia is a live engagement tool: hosts create and run real-time interactive sessions — quiz, poll, flashcard deck, presentation, course, or graded exam — that a group joins instantly via a code or QR, with an instant leaderboard and arcade energy. Success is two-sided and equally weighted: the live session itself has to go off without friction (players join instantly, no lag, no confusion, the moment feels polished and fun), and the content a host builds has to be worth keeping and reusing session after session, not a one-off throwaway.

## Positioning

Brivia is the one tool that covers quiz, poll, flashcard, presentation, course, and exam in a single product with arcade-level fun and real depth — where Kahoot, Mentimeter, and Wooclap each cover one or two of those and stop. Every screen should reinforce that breadth-without-compromise: playful in the live moment, rigorous where it needs to be (grading, question banks, exam attempts).

## Brand Personality

Playful, energetic, bold — trust the existing Arcade Pop visual system (vibrant violet `#7048ff`, warm cream paper background, rounded game-like shapes) as the personality baseline. Voice should feel like a host hyping up a room, not a SaaS product explaining itself.

## Anti-references

Not corporate SaaS-cream: avoid the templated, soulless enterprise-dashboard look (flat gray-on-beige cards, generic icon grids). The arcade energy is the differentiator — losing it to "professional" blandness undercuts the whole positioning.

## Design Principles

- **Breadth without compromise**: every surface should read as capable of both a 30-second icebreaker poll and a graded exam — never tip fully into "toy" or fully into "enterprise tool."
- **The live moment is sacred**: session join, leaderboard, and real-time sync are the product's proof point — never let secondary UI (settings, builders) slow down or visually compete with in-session screens.
- **Built content should invite reuse**: builders (quiz, course, exam, question bank) should make hosts want to keep and refine their library, not just ship one-off sessions.
- **Fun by default, precise on demand**: playful visual language (Arcade Pop) everywhere by default, with room for a more serious register (Thales theme) where grading and rigor matter.
- **One host, many contexts**: don't design for "the teacher" or "the corporate host" specifically — design for whoever is standing in front of a group with a code to share.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline: contrast, full keyboard navigation, and a respected reduced-motion preference across both host and player views, including live in-session screens.
